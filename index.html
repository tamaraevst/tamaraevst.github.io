I am a PhD student at Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics at the University of Cambridge. I specialise in Numerical Relativity and Tests of General Relativity using gravitational waves. I am part of an amazing numerical relativity code collaboration GRChombo. GRChombo is a finite difference code and is built on the Chombo adaptive mesh refinement (AMR) libraries. I am also a member of LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA collaboration, which made the first observation of the gravitational wave event GW150914 from a binary black hole merger.

My work concerns simualtions of binary systems mainly consisting of black holes and boson stars. Understading the dynamics in modified theories of gravity and the effects of "new matter", like scalar fields, have been the primary foci of my studies. The list of my publications can be found here.
